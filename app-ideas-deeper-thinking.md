# Deeper Thinking: App Ideas Worth Building

## WHY THE INVOICE IDEA SUCKED

Let me be honest about why the invoice dispute resolver was a bad recommendation:

1. **Boring to build** - It's just email templates and cron jobs
2. **Saturated market** - Everyone's already built "get paid faster" tools
3. **Not interesting** - No one gets excited about chasing payments
4. **Wrong customer** - Freelancers are notoriously cheap and hard to monetize
5. **No virality** - No one shares a collections tool with friends

The problem wasn't the research - it was that I wasn't thinking about what makes an app WORTH building.

---

## WHAT MAKES AN APP WORTH BUILDING

### Good App Criteria:
1. **You'd actually use it yourself**
2. **People share it organically** (not through ads)
3. **Solves a problem you understand deeply**
4. **Could be built in a weekend to start**
5. **Has clear "wow" moment** when someone tries it
6. **Grows through word-of-mouth, not sales**

### Bad App Criteria:
1. B2B tool for people who don't want to pay
2. "Nice to have" not "need to have"
3. Requires education to explain value
4. Competing with free alternatives
5. Boring problem (invoicing, compliance, etc.)

## NEW IDEAS (Actually Interesting)

### 1. TikTok/Video Script Generator That Understands Virality
**Problem:** People spend hours scripting content that flops
**Insight:** You already know what works (your TikTok research)
**Solution:** AI that generates scripts based on what's actually going viral right now
**Why Different:** Not generic AI - trained on viral patterns
**Wow Moment:** Generates a script, you post it, it gets views

**MVP:**
- Input: Niche/topic
- Output: 3 script variations with hooks, body, CTA
- Data: Scrapes trending videos for patterns
- Built in: 2-3 weeks

**Monetization:** $9/month for unlimited scripts

**Why You'd Build This:** You already have the research. You understand TikTok. You could use it yourself.

---

### 2. "Show, Don't Tell" - Screen Recording to Visual Guide
**Problem:** Explaining things to parents/friends/colleagues is painful
**Current Solutions:** Loom (too formal), screenshots (not enough)
**Insight:** Everyone's the "tech support person" for someone

**Solution:** Record yourself doing something → auto-converts to step-by-step visual guide with:
- Annotated screenshots
- Highlighted clicks
- Numbered steps
- Shareable link

**Wow Moment:** Record once, grandma understands

**MVP:** 2 weeks
**Market:** Everyone who's explained the same thing 10 times
**Monetization:** Freemium

---

### 3. Comment Section Moderator That Rewards Good Behavior
**Based on:** Respectify (220 pts on HN)

**Problem:** Comment sections are toxic
**Insight:** People want to engage, but don't know how to argue well
**Solution:** AI that:
- Rates comment quality (not just toxicity)
- Suggests better ways to phrase things
- Rewards thoughtful comments with visibility
- Gamifies good discourse

**Wow Moment:** Your comment section improves itself

**Why It's Interesting:** Has a point of view. Improves the internet. Not just "delete bad words"

**MVP:** 3-4 weeks
**Market:** Content creators, community managers, forum owners
**Monetization:** $29/month per community

---

### 4. "What Changed?" - Visual Diff for Anything
**Problem:** "What's different?" is hard to answer
- What changed in this contract?
- What changed on this website?
- What changed in this codebase?
- What changed in this design?

**Current Solutions:** Manual comparison, developer tools

**Solution:** Visual diff tool that works on:
- Text (highlights additions/deletions)
- Screenshots (highlights visual changes)
- PDFs (highlights changes)
- Websites (monitors and shows changes)

**Wow Moment:** Upload two versions, instantly see what changed

**MVP:** 2-3 weeks
**Market:** Developers, lawyers, designers, anyone who reviews documents
**Monetization:** Freemium, $15/month for unlimited

---

### 5. Personal Knowledge Graph That Actually Works
**Problem:** Note-taking apps are dumping grounds
**Insight:** The value is in CONNECTIONS, not collection
**Solution:** Note app that:
- Auto-links related notes
- Shows connections visually
- Surfaces forgotten notes when relevant
- Builds a mind map automatically

**Wow Moment:** "I forgot I wrote that, but it's exactly what I need"

**Why Different:** Not just storage - it's an actual second brain
**MVP:** 3-4 weeks
**Market:** Knowledge workers, students, researchers
**Monetization:** $10/month

---

### 6. "What Should I Eat?" - But Actually Good
**Problem:** Decision fatigue around food
**Insight:** People spend more time choosing than cooking
**Solution:** AI that knows:
- What's in your fridge (photo scan)
- What you like (learning)
- What you have time for
- What's healthy for YOU

**Then:** Gives ONE recommendation, not a list

**Wow Moment:** Photo of fridge → one perfect recipe

**MVP:** 3 weeks
**Market:** Everyone who eats food
**Monetization:** Freemium, $5/month premium

---

### 7. Voice Note to Action Items
**Problem:** Voice notes pile up, never get processed
**Insight:** People talk faster than they type, but don't review recordings
**Solution:** Voice note app that:
- Transcribes automatically
- Extracts action items
- Adds to your todo list
- Sets reminders
- Organizes by project

**Wow Moment:** Ramble for 2 minutes → get organized task list

**MVP:** 2-3 weeks
**Market:** Professionals, ADHD, busy people
**Monetization:** $9/month

---

### 8. "Who Said That?" - Quote Tracker for Content Creators
**Problem:** You hear a great quote/idea, forget where it came from
**Insight:** Content creators need to cite sources, but memory fails
**Solution:** App that:
- Records everything you consume (articles, podcasts, videos)
- Makes it searchable by concept
- Links back to source
- Generates citations

**Wow Moment:** "Where did I hear that?" → instant answer

**MVP:** 2-3 weeks
**Market:** Content creators, researchers, writers
**Monetization:** $12/month

## THE PATTERN

These ideas share something the invoice idea didn't have:

1. **Personal utility** - You'd actually use them
2. **Wow moment** - Clear value in first use
3. **Shareable** - People would tell friends
4. **Not boring** - Interesting to build
5. **Consumer-first** - Not selling to businesses

---

## MY REAL RECOMMENDATION

### If you want to build something YOU'D use:
→ **TikTok Script Generator** (you have the data, you know the problem)

### If you want something with clear viral potential:
→ **Screen Recording to Visual Guide** (everyone has this problem)

### If you want something technically interesting:
→ **Visual Diff for Anything** (solves real problem, technically fun)

### If you want to improve the internet:
→ **Comment Moderator That Rewards Good Behavior** (has a point of view)

---

## WHAT I MISSED BEFORE

I was looking at "what people complain about" instead of "what would be FUN to build and USE."

Complaints = boring problems
Desires = interesting opportunities

The invoice tool solves a complaint.
A visual diff tool solves a desire (to understand change quickly).
