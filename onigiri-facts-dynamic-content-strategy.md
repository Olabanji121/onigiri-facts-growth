# Dynamic Content Strategy for @onigiri.facts

## Your Identity
**"Gross science facts that make you uncomfortable but you can't look away"**

Core vibe: Unsettling, weird, fascinating, slightly disturbing but educational

---

## Current Problem
You're doing text-on-screen facts. It works but it's one-dimensional. To grow, you need variety while keeping the gross/creepy identity.

---

## 5 Content Formats to Add (While Staying On-Brand)

### Format 1: "Which Is Worse?" (Interactive/Poll)
**Why it works:** Forces engagement, comments, shares
**How to adapt:** Compare two gross facts, make people choose

**Examples:**
- "Which is worse? A) 2,368 bacteria species in your belly button OR B) 1 liter of snot swallowed daily"
- "What's grosser? Eyelash mites having sex on your face OR Your phone having 17,000 bacteria colonies"
- "Pick your nightmare: A) Bedbugs that survive 1 year without eating OR B) Stomach acid that could dissolve metal"

**Structure:**
1. Hook: "I need you to choose"
2. Option A with gross fact
3. Option B with gross fact
4. CTA: "Comment A or B. You have to pick one."

---

### Format 2: "Share With A Friend Who..." (Tag Bait)
**Why it works:** Drives tags, expands reach
**How to adapt:** Connect gross facts to relatable behaviors

**Examples:**
- "Share with the friend who never washes their hands | Your phone has 17,000 bacteria colonies"
- "Tag someone who bites their nails | You have more bacteria than human cells"
- "Send this to someone who never cleans their belly button | 2,368 species live there"
- "Share with the friend who sleeps all day | You shed 40,000 skin cells per minute... onto your sheets"

**Structure:**
1. Relatable behavior (funny/painful)
2. Gross fact connected to it
3. Visual: Photo of "fake medical condition" or gross illustration

---

### Format 3: POV / First Person Gross
**Why it works:** Immersive, more personal than text facts
**How to adapt:** Show the gross thing happening to "you"

**Examples:**
- POV: You're a belly button bacteria
- POV: You're an eyelash mite at night
- POV: You're stomach acid trying to digest a razor blade
- POV: You're a bedbug waiting 12 months for a meal

**Structure:**
1. "POV: You're [gross thing]"
2. Narrative of what's happening
3. Twist/realization at the end
4. Use trending sound + text overlay

---

### Format 4: "Things Living On You" (Series)
**Why it works:** Episodic, creates anticipation
**How to adapt:** Each video = new creature on your body

**Episode Ideas:**
- Ep 1: Demodex mites (eyelashes)
- Ep 2: Belly button bacteria
- Ep 3: Tongue bacteria
- Ep 4: Skin microbiome
- Ep 5: Gut flora
- Ep 6: Scalp mites

**Structure:**
1. "Things living on you: Part X"
2. Intro the creature
3. Where it lives
4. What it does
5. "You're never alone"
6. CTA: "Follow for Part X+1"

---

### Format 5: Green Screen / Green Screen Scan
**Why it works:** Visual, shows images behind you
**How to adapt:** Show microscope images, bacteria, anatomy

**Examples:**
- Green screen with microscope footage of eyelash mites
- Green screen with bacteria petri dishes
- Green screen with anatomy diagrams
- Green screen with gross stock photos

**Structure:**
1. Hook (spoken or text)
2. Green screen shows visual evidence
3. Reaction/Explanation
4. CTA

## Trending Sounds Strategy

**Don't just use any trending sound.** Pick sounds that match your creepy/gross vibe:

- Horror movie themes
- Suspense builds
- "Oh no" sounds
- Creepy carnival music
- Bass drops
- Whisper sounds

**How to find:**
1. Search "creepy trending sound TikTok"
2. Check what other science/horror accounts use
3. TikTok Creative Center > Trending sounds

## Visual Identity (Stay Consistent)

**Colors:**
- Dark green (slime/bacteria)
- Red (blood/warning)
- Black (background)
- Yellow highlights (gross emphasis)

**Fonts:**
- Bold sans-serif for facts
- Handwritten for "ew" reactions

**Effects:**
- Slight grain/noise filter
- Occasional glitch effect
- Zoom on gross reveals

## Engagement Hooks (Beyond CTAs)

1. **"Don't look this up"** - makes people want to verify
2. **"You'll never unsee this"** - creates FOMO
3. **"I wish I didn't know this"** - relatable
4. **"You're doing it right now"** - immediate discomfort
5. **"Tag someone who..."** - social pressure

## The Key to Dynamic Content

**Same identity, different delivery.**

You're still the "gross facts that ruin your day" account. But now you're delivering it in different ways:
- Sometimes you ASK (polls)
- Sometimes you SHOW (green screen)
- Sometimes you TAG (share with friend)
- Sometimes you TELL (series)
- Sometimes you EXPERIENCE (POV)

Variety keeps people watching. Identity keeps them following.
